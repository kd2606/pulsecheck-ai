# Design Decisions: DiagnoVerse

## Shift to a Trust-First, Calm, Clinical UI

### Background
Initially, the project might have leaned towards standard, vibrant hackathon-style templates. However, considering our target demographic (rural India) and our domain (healthcare diagnostics), we have pivoted to a completely different aesthetic.

### Core Philosophy
1. **Trust-First**: Healthcare applications must instantly evoke a sense of reliability and safety. Users and healthcare workers need to trust the tool with sensitive health data.
2. **Calm & Clinical**: The design will utilize a calm color palette—whites, soft blues, and muted grays—avoiding overly bright or distracting elements. This clinical feel mimics professional healthcare environments.
3. **Accessibility over Flash**: The UI prioritizes clear typography, high contrast for legibility on lower-end devices, and straightforward navigation. Flashy animations and complex layouts are discarded in favor of performance and clarity.

### Implementation Goals
- Drop all generic 'hackathon' templates and flashy components.
- Establish a minimalist design system in our UI, ensuring it feels robust and serious.
- Ensure all patient-facing and worker-facing interfaces are intuitive, requiring minimal onboarding.
- Adhere to legal and safety messaging consistently (e.g., clear medical disclaimers, DPDP Act 2023 compliance notices).
